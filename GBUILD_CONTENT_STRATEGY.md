# GBuild Solutions - Content Strategy

**Date:** 2026-06-26

## 1. Content objective

The content should help non-technical and semi-technical decision-makers understand:

- what GBuild Solutions does
- whether it fits their problem
- why they should trust it
- what to do next

## 2. Content principles

- simple
- direct
- professional
- concrete
- business-readable
- low on buzzwords

## 3. Homepage hero direction

### Role

Create fast clarity.

### Message direction

Do not open with abstract AI claims.

Open with:

- digital systems
- software
- workflows
- business modernization

### Hero formula

- headline: what we build
- subheadline: who it helps and why it matters
- CTA: start the conversation

## 4. Service descriptions

Each service should explain:

- the business problem
- what GBuild Solutions builds or changes
- who the service is for
- what the outcome looks like

Avoid service descriptions that are only lists of tools or technologies.

## 5. About section direction

The About page should answer:

- why this company exists
- what kind of partner it is
- how it relates to the broader ecosystem

Tone:

- clear and credible
- not overdramatic
- not overly personal unless the founder wants that

## 6. Process content direction

Process copy should reduce buyer anxiety.

It should answer:

- what happens first
- what the client receives
- where approvals happen
- how scope becomes real work

Recommended tone:

- calm
- structured
- reassuring

## 7. Why choose us direction

This section should not rely on fake scale claims.

It should focus on:

- practical systems thinking
- clear communication
- execution capability
- cross-domain technical depth
- business-aware implementation

## 8. AI solutions content direction

The AI page should frame AI as useful and grounded.

Preferred angles:

- workflow improvement
- document handling
- internal productivity
- decision support
- business process automation

Avoid:

- AGI-style claims
- generic "AI transformation" language with no explanation

## 9. Digital transformation content direction

This page should translate digitization into ordinary business pain:

- too much manual follow-up
- disconnected tools
- weak reporting
- slow internal processes
- customer handling spread across many channels

The page should make the problem feel familiar and the solution feel structured.

## 10. Case-study content direction

Do not exaggerate.

For each case study, structure the story as:

- client or context
- challenge
- what was built
- why the approach made sense
- current outcome or project status

Use placeholders where public outcome proof is missing.

## 11. Contact CTA direction

Contact copy should feel low-friction and serious.

Suggested direction:

- tell us what you are trying to improve
- whether it is a website, internal system, platform, or AI workflow
- we will help you clarify the next step

## 12. Editorial themes for blog / insights

- how SMEs can digitize operations without overbuilding
- when a website should become a platform
- practical AI use cases for real businesses
- how to scope a useful internal tool
- common mistakes in software outsourcing
- how to prepare for a software project as a non-technical founder

## 13. Language rules

Prefer:

- build
- improve
- organize
- digitize
- streamline
- structure
- support

Avoid excessive repetition of:

- transform
- innovate
- disrupt
- leverage
- cutting-edge

## 14. Content needs still missing

- approved public company description
- founder bio decision
- public project list
- approved testimonials if any
- initial language choice for launch
