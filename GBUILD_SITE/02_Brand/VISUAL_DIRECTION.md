# GBuild Solutions - Visual Direction

## Overall Direction

Create a premium B2B software aesthetic with strong typography, clean structural rhythm, selective asymmetry, and restrained but intelligent motion. The design should feel more like a high-end product company than a general web agency.

## Option A - Dark Premium AI/Software

Colors:

- deep graphite or near-black surfaces
- soft off-white text
- controlled teal or mineral accent
- muted metallic neutral details

Emotional effect:

- advanced
- cinematic
- premium
- technically powerful

Best use case:

- if the brand wants maximum sophistication and a more immersive, high-end feel

Risks:

- can look too trend-driven or “cool startup” if the typography and copy are not disciplined
- harder to keep lightness and business readability across long content pages

## Option B - Clean Light B2B SaaS

Colors:

- cloud off-white base
- graphite text
- teal accent
- stone dividers
- restrained sand detail tone

Emotional effect:

- trustworthy
- clear
- calm
- business-readable

Best use case:

- if the launch priority is conversion clarity and broad credibility across SMEs, startups, and institutions

Risks:

- can drift toward safe/generic B2B if the layout and motion are not distinctive enough

## Option C - Hybrid Dark + Light Sections

Colors:

- light editorial base for most pages
- deep dark sections for hero, process emphasis, or CTA chapters
- teal accent carried consistently across both modes

Emotional effect:

- premium
- modern
- layered
- more dynamic than all-light

Best use case:

- if the brand wants both clarity and drama without committing fully to a dark site

Risks:

- harder to maintain consistency if section transitions are not designed carefully
- can feel patchy if dark sections are used without clear narrative purpose

## Recommended Option

Recommend **Option C: Hybrid Dark + Light Sections**.

Why:

- it balances trust and sophistication
- it lets the hero and selected proof/process sections feel premium and advanced
- it keeps service and content pages readable and SEO-friendly
- it avoids both generic all-light SaaS and over-stylized all-dark AI branding

## What To Avoid

- purple-blue neon palettes
- generic AI glows
- full-page beige editorial treatment
- decorative 3D objects with no business meaning
- repeated identical feature cards
